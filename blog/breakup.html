Lessons from Breakup

Bad things happen to good people. I did everything right and yet she still left me because she wanted to "work on herself".

It taught me that I could do everything right and still "lose".

I am more self-focused now, able to spend more time on things and people that truly matter.

Although deep down, it affects me not to be with someone — sometimes there's an empty feeling of doing everything for a small group that doesn't include a partner.

I've learned that we can't change anyone — change has to come from within.

People are temporary, and at any moment they can turn around and leave. Some fake feelings, and many just want to take advantage of us.

I've learned not to create expectations of others, and to give back in equal measure. I've set clear limits and won't let anyone cross them again.

I trust my instincts more and let go of those who show they want to leave. I'll never tolerate disrespect again.

Since the breakup, I've changed. I've become colder, more calculating. I've developed a kind of repulsion toward love and dating.

Also, the romantic part of me went quiet — something in me shut down after she left.

I used to be a super romantic person, giving gifts, making surprises, now I'm the opposite. I've also become more physically distant, I used to love kissing, hugging and being with people all day, now I can't and I'm much more distant.

But overall, between what I've learned and how I've changed, the scales are tipping in my favor. I'm a much more aware person with my feet on the ground.
